Control

Flight Control System Development

LQR Controller Design

A Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR) was developed for longitudinal stability augmentation and trajectory tracking. The controller was designed using state-space representation of the aircraft dynamics, with careful tuning of the cost function matrices to balance performance and control effort.

Control System Architecture

The control system consists of multiple layers: inner loop for stability augmentation using LQR, middle loop for attitude control, and outer loop for trajectory tracking. The system was designed to maintain stable flight and recover from disturbances while following commanded flight paths.

Manufacturing Process

Wing Fabrication

Wings constructed using expanded polystyrene (EPS) foam shaped with CNC hot-wire cutter following NACA 4418 profile. Carbon fiber spars added for structural reinforcement.

Fuselage Construction

Fuselage built using lightweight balsa wood framework with foam core, covered with heat-shrink film for aerodynamic smoothness and protection.

Control Surface Installation

Ailerons, elevator, and rudder cut from wing/tail sections and connected via hinges. Servo motors installed with linkages for precise control surface actuation.

Systems Integration

Installation of flight controller, ESC, receiver, servos, battery, and wiring. Careful attention to weight distribution to achieve proper center of gravity location.

Final Assembly & Finishing

Complete aircraft assembly with final adjustments. Application of protective coatings and surface finish. Installation of surveillance camera payload.

Validation

Testing & Results

Ground Testing

Comprehensive ground tests were conducted to validate all systems before flight attempts:

  • Control Surface Check: Verified proper servo operation and control surface deflections
  • CG Verification: Confirmed center of gravity within acceptable range (25-30% MAC)
  • Motor/Propulsion Test: Validated thrust output and battery endurance
  • Systems Integration: Tested communication between flight controller, receiver, and actuators
  • Pre-flight Checklist: Systematic verification of all critical parameters
Pre-flight ground testing and systems validation

Flight Testing

Flight testing revealed critical insights into the practical challenges of fixed-wing UAV operation. While the aircraft exhibited good aerodynamic characteristics based on simulations, several factors contributed to difficulties during actual flight:

Key Observations

  • Launch Phase: Hand-launch technique required significant practice and coordination
  • Stability: Aircraft showed sensitivity to CG position and trim settings
  • Control Response: Need for careful gain tuning to avoid PIO (Pilot Induced Oscillations)
  • Environmental Factors: Wind conditions significantly affected flight characteristics
Flight testing and launch attempts

Lessons Learned

Technical Insights

  • Importance of precise CG location
  • Need for extensive ground testing
  • Critical role of structural rigidity
  • Value of progressive testing approach

Operational Lessons

  • Pilot training and experience crucial
  • Environmental assessment essential
  • Backup systems and redundancy important
  • Iterative refinement necessary

While the flight testing phase encountered challenges, these provided invaluable learning experiences. The crash analysis revealed specific areas for improvement in future iterations, particularly regarding structural reinforcement, control system tuning, and pre-flight procedures.
